A new paradigm in software development

Conventional software development involves unlimited options and unrestricted power, which is invariably paired with high complexity, responsiblity, and cost. There are few second- or third-tier options for less-technical, would-be software authors. Thus, a lot of people miss out on the productivity gains of custom software solutions, which are either outside their budget or are not worth developing.

We envision a new paradigm of software development - one that boils down the high-level components of an application into building blocks that non-programmers can assemble and maintain. This new paradigm will break down the barriers of entry by reducing complexity and setup, while tailoring terms and tutorials to a conceptual level that doesnâ€™t require tomes of technical knowledge.

This new paradigm also aims at standarizing the process and tools of the software development process. The current methods are not optimized for collaboration - especially not the interdisciplinary kind. The new paradigm offers the possibility of such collaboration. Instead of product owners, business analysts, and designers producing artifacts to be disseminated and implemented by an engineer, the entire team may use the tool to materially participate in the process of software development.
